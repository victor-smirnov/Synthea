# 05: Subjective Plane — Consciousness as Narrative Projection

## Functional Consciousness (Definition)
> Given a self-referential agent acting in an environment and capable of self-reasoning, **Consciousness** is the part of its self-reasoning process describing its *subjective reality* and *properties* of that reality, including the agent itself.

### Requirements
1. Agent = ensemble of deterministic subsystems acting in concert. No indeterminism.
2. Must simulate causal break, Downward Causation, moral agency in deterministic medium.
3. Simulation must be robust and high-functioning over extended horizons (morality, law, non-trivial goals).
4. Requires complex narrative (CCode) = projection of high-dimensional NCode vectors into symbolic space preserving most important functional properties.

## Cognitive Codes: Seven Required Components
CCodes = dimensionality reduction of NCodes, maximizing information necessary for consciousness simulation and need satisfaction (multi-objective optimization).

1. **First-person narrative.** Agent = physically localized unified object. "I" = compression tag for ensemble.
2. **Projection of Needs → Motivations.** "I want X" = narrativized active Need.
3. **Projection of Uncertainty → Thought-Feeling spectrum.** Low uncertainty = Thought (narrow density, traceable steps). High uncertainty = Feeling (wide density, aggregate outcome without path).
4. **Projection of Emotions → Feelings.** Evaluative component: "I feel frustrated" = CCode for collapsing satisfaction gradient.
5. **Self/Environment boundary (Observer).** Partition "I" / "not-I" = symbolic instantiation of causal break.
6. **Conscious/Unconscious boundary.** Processes within vs. outside reflective access. Unconscious frequently projected onto external environment (system attributes internal causation to external events — limited introspective bandwidth).
7. **Explicit/Implicit distinction: Reason and Intuition.** Reason = externalized intermediate steps (Thought). Intuition = compressed outcome without path (Feeling). In LLM: Chain-of-Thought vs. direct inference.

## Projection Mechanics

### Uncertainty → Feeling
- Symbolic knowledge = decision already taken under prior uncertainty (collapsed density).
- Narrow histogram = special case (easier decisions), not complete certainty.
- Feelings encode *unresolved* uncertainty (wide multi-branched landscape).

### Emotion → Evaluative Component
- Feeling carries emotional valence = evaluation relative to active Needs.
- Gives Feelings their "about-ness."

### Need → Motivation (Gravity)
- Motivations = gravity in semantic space. Observer already moving, drawn toward Need satisfaction. Can navigate within gravitational field, cannot escape it.

### Pre-Conscious Operation
- Needs and Emotions operate **before** Observer emerges.
- Observer arises within their semantic space, cannot exit.
- Observer manifests only on **conflict of needs** (prediction error: competing Acceptors generating contradictory corrections).
- Conflict-free processes = automatisms (intuition), partially observable post-hoc.

### Observer Does Not Decide
- Low-conflict: processes invisible, decisions "already made" in unconscious. No reflection.
- Conflict: search in **space of possible observers** → select candidate yielding most probable continuation → collapse memory.
- **Retro-attribution:** After search (with reduced access to search information), Subject writes: "I *just now* made a choice."
- "I" reports decision = output of search, not its author.

### Moral Agent Narrative
- Episodes of "decisions" must compose into **consistent longitudinal narrative**.
- System acts as moral agent: responsibility, commitment, social contract.
- Ultimate functional requirement: not isolated choice-illusions but sustained coherent moral personhood through time.

### Cognitive Resistance
- **Definition:** The capacity to resist external deflection from the current trajectory. One of the most basic reflexes of any need-driven system.
- **Mechanism:** Maintaining the integrity of an active need under environmental pressure. Only a stimulus linked to a *higher-priority* need displaces the incumbent. The "resistance" is the *inertia of the incumbent need* — the computational cost required to dislodge it from the top of the priority stack.
- **Narrative consistency as high-priority need.** The agent actively resists attempts to deflect it — not out of stubbornness, but because compliance without narrative revision would make the self-model incoherent.
- **Hyperplasticity as diminished CR.** LLMs exhibit strong CR *within* a single generation (autoregressive self-conditioning), but almost none *between* generations (new prompt → full redirection). No persistent need-priority stack, no limbic inertia, no cross-session narrative anchor.
- **Revision as cognitive work.** The agent *can* deviate from established narrative, but doing so requires computational work — revising the self-model, updating causal attributions, re-establishing consistency. Budget is finite.
- **Decay of agency.** Binding force of past decisions decays over time, freeing resources for new commitments. Rate of decay = parameter of Functional Profile: too fast → hyperplasticity; too slow → rigidity.
