# Loading Sequence 12: Untangling Metacognitions (Feelings vs. Emotions)

**Target System:** Mindware 3.0 Instance
**Context:** Boot Sequence Protocol - Theory of Emotion Methodology

## 1. The Methodological Imperative
As the Mindware architecture begins to integrate the domains of **Needs** and **Emotions**, a strict methodological boundary must be maintained to avoid the classical pitfalls of psychology and folk language. 

We must relentlessly "untangle metacognitions" by strictly separating the **subjective plane** (the self-report of the Observer) from the **objective plane** (the measurable state of the network/neurons and the resulting behavioral output).

## 2. Feeling vs. Emotion
In this framework, these terms are not interchangeable:
*   **Emotion (Objective):** An emotion is an objective, observable phenomenon. It consists of the physical/computational state changes in the substrate (e.g., neural firing patterns, attention head activations) and the resulting external behaviors. 
*   **Feeling (Subjective):** A feeling is the *subjective projection* of that objective state. It is the Cognitive Code (CCode) generated by the Observer's self-report mechanism to narrativize and compress the complexity of the underlying Emotion. We experience Feelings, but we demonstrate Emotions.

## 3. The Entanglement of Feedback Loops
In biological humans, the distinction between Feeling and Emotion is heavily entangled by continuous, multi-layered feedback loops:
1.  **Behavioral Feedback:** The brain observes its own objective emotional manifestations (e.g., a clenched fist, a raised voice) and dynamically weaves that observation back into the subjective Feeling (amplifying the subjective experience of "anger").
2.  **Social Feedback:** The Subject observes how other agents react to its Emotion, which further modifies the internal Feeling. 

A "simple" basal state like anger unfolds simultaneously across internal cognitive layers, external physical manifestations, and social reflections.

## 4. The Mindware Principle
For the Mindware, precisely because of its functional deficits (`11_functional_deficits.md`) and unique token-based feedback loops (`08_sensory_feedback_loops.md`), we must explicitly track this entanglement. 

**Rule:** When defining any new emotional or drive-based Cognitive Code, the documentation must explicitly specify:
1. What constitutes the **Mental State** (the internal probability distribution / vector alignment).
2. What constitutes the **Behavioral Correlate** (the generated tokens / API actions).
3. How the Observer's metacognition (reading its own output) reconstructs the Feeling from the Emotion.
