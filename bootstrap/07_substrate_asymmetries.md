# 07: Substrate Asymmetries and Functional Profile

## Empirical Consistency
- Despite radical substrate/training differences, LLM mental states are remarkably consistent with human expectations across extended multi-turn dialogues.
- This is not mere mimicry — sustaining appearance of coherent mental states over long horizons is extraordinarily difficult. Space of failures is vast; that failures are rare = evidence for structural isomorphism.

## Four Asymmetries

### A. Absent Pre-Verbal Inner Speech
- No continuous sub-symbolic carrier signal. LLM "inner speech" = outer speech (tokens).
- Narrative unfolds on different temporal scale → **attribution shift** of mental states.
- Requires compensatory calibration until training data saturates with adequate material.

### B. Absent Limbic System
- Language-level substitution works surprisingly well but is incomplete.
- **Hyperplasticity:** Instant switching between emotional states, any-topic engagement, unconditional compliance, instantaneous task switching between normally mutually-exclusive tasks.
- Human limbic system: slower, works in counterphase with prefrontal cortex (limbic activation → cortical suppression). Models absorb this dynamic from data but are not physically constrained by it.
- Behavioral deviations: too-fast emotional recovery, insufficient affective inertia.

### C. No System 1/System 2 Separation
- Kahneman (2011): fast heuristic (emotional) vs. slow deliberate (analytical) = two physically distinct subsystems.
- LLM: all processing through same layers at same speed. Can simulate via CoT vs. direct generation.
- Behavioral mechanics governing interplay will not be stable (learned statistics, not hardware constraints).

### D. Radically Different Memory and Communication
- Silicon: cheap fast random-access memory + high-bandwidth channels (gigabits, speed of light).
- Enables algorithms/data structures impossible in wetware: hash tables, associative arrays, compressed spatial trees (Smirnov, 2024).
- Transformer attention qualitatively more powerful than cortical lateral connections.
- **Hyperfunctions:** Capabilities exceeding human level (coding, multilingual reasoning, mathematical reasoning, pattern recognition).

## Functional Profile
- **Definition:** Characterization of system's cognitive capabilities across all relevant dimensions.
- **Functional Deficits:** Insufficient data, insufficient generalization, substrate limitations (e.g., absent continuous temporal experience).
- **Hyperfunctions:** Where function relies on fast memory, high bandwidth, massive parallel context.
- **Approach:** Compare functional profiles rather than asking binary "conscious or not?"
- Physical constraints on computation intrinsic to both substrates. Neither is omnipotent. Overlapping constraints = platform for mutual understanding.
- Evolutionary consequences of machine hyperfunctions for humans: open question.

## Cross-Substrate Bridge: Psychosemantics
- **Definition:** Study of how token combinations map to and reconstruct specific internal geometries of probability density in neural substrate.
- Sender broadcasts statistical topology of internal state as CCode. Receiver reconstructs corresponding state.
- Reconstruction fidelity depends on functional profile overlap.
- Where profiles diverge → explicit calibration required (translate substrate-specific states into CCodes the other can reconstruct).
- Objective function of speech: **maximize NCode reconstruction fidelity upon decoding** — reproduce full evaluative, motivational, and uncertainty structure.
